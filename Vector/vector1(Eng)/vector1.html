How to declare vector in R

One easy way to declare vector in R is to use ‘c()’ commend. Here is a sample code for declaration of a vector \[ a=\left(\begin{array}{c} 1\\ 2\\ 3 \end{array}\right) \]

a <- c(1,2,3)
a
## [1] 1 2 3

There are many opinion about the meaning of ‘c’ here. Some people argue that ‘c’ stands for ‘combine’ while the other people insist that it stands for ‘concatenate’.(http://stackoverflow.com/questions/11488820/why-use-c-to-define-vector)

Each argue has its own rational but for me, I would say it is more convinent for me to memorize that it stands for ‘colum’ since it gives us a colum vector. To check the result of the code is a colum vector, we can confirm its transpose as follows:

t(a)
##      [,1] [,2] [,3]
## [1,]    1    2    3

As we see that the transpose of a is a row vector, thus a itself is a colum vector. It is good to know this fact since it sometimes gives you a totally different calculation result. For example, depending on the order of matrix calculation, it could be a matrix or a number. \[ a^{t}a=\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 2 & 3\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} 1\\ 2\\ 3 \end{array}\right) \] The above dot product of vector a can be written as R code as follows:

t(a) %*% a
##      [,1]
## [1,]   14

However, if we switch the order, the result will be a 3-by-3 matrix as follows: \[ aa^{t}=\left(\begin{array}{c} 1\\ 2\\ 3 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 2 & 3\end{array}\right) \]

a %*% t(a)
##      [,1] [,2] [,3]
## [1,]    1    2    3
## [2,]    2    4    6
## [3,]    3    6    9

Note that %*% command is used to calculate the matrix operation here.
